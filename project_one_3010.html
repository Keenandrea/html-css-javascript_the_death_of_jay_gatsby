  • The Invitation

    The Death of Jay Gatsby took place on a Wednesday morning in September 1922. Invitations were sent out well in advance. The invitations read:

    Mr. Jay Gatsby, The Great

    Affluential Host and Undisclosed Bootlegger

    Requests the Honor of Your Presence

    On the Occasion of his Demise

    West Egg 3:20 p.m.

    Snacks will be Provided

    RSVP by 5th of September

  • Public Response

    One can imagine the feelings of the recipients. Our great Gatsby! Preparing to quit the world! and so on. Mr. Great Gatsby! Who has given us so much shindiggery! and so and so. Verily, a fine example of human achievement. But on the whole, it has to be said, a tragic mishap arranging to come about, like a whale, beached, dehydrating, collapsing under its own weight, or drowning when high tide covers the blowhole.

  • Preparation

    People prepared to attend the death of Jay Gatsby as they might have for a mandatory staff meeting. There were debates as to whether the object of their being there ought to matter. Did any among them establish a kinship with Mr. Gatsby? was a the lacktherof enough? By September 5th, a mountain of invitations, down to the last, explicitly containing names, were returned, each including its own lengthy justification for unattendance.

  • Nick's Furor

    Nick Carraway, by a very long chalk, demonstrated the most furor. Enough furor to blow a house down. Jackals! he continued to shriek aloud while pasing the sumptuous chamber, flipping antique furniture, leaving in his wake a path of upside-down antique furniture. Leeches! A cuss on their mouldy hearts! (yanking an antique Edwardian endtable out from under itself [floral arrangement set atop endtable spilling fantastically everywhere.]). A coterie of stewards, having nothing better to do, trailed closely behind Mr. Carraway, reinstituting Carraway's displacement of furniture. Whether this was for the benefit of the chamber and its altogether altogetherness, or for the longevity of Mr. Carroway's furor within the chamber, remains untold.

  • The Great Gatsby

    Jay Gatsby himself waited, in a leather smoking chair, which, contrary to the popular naming convention, was neither on fire, nor smoking. He looked extremely tired, yet calm, assuming a very blue look. Almost indigo, was his look. And it never changed color. Not once throughout his death. Not even when Nick Carraway suspended his furor to perform impressions for Gatsby, impressions of famous contemporary film stars: Rudolph Valentino, Harold Lloyd, Harry Houdini, and Gatsby's favorite, Charlie Chaplin. Not even then. Gatsby remained posed, silent before the shut window, ruefully gazing eatward.

  • That's it

    And there, east, across the dark body of water, several electricians deployed by Ameren UE, stood in joint scrutiny round the foot of a mighty metal pole, where, on the tippy-top, a single green bulb, in a state of disrepair, signaled nothing for nobody.
